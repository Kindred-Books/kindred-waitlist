Building the next generation of children's books

The problem

Every night, millions of parents read their children books about characters and worlds that have nothing to do with their family, their memories, or who their child is becoming. The books are generic. The science says that's a missed opportunity.

Children remember stories about themselves 3-4 times better than other stories. The way parents narrate children's experiences directly shapes their autobiographical memory, identity development, and emotional resilience. This is well-established research — Prof. Robyn Fivush at Emory University has spent decades documenting it. Nobody has built the product that acts on it.

The solution

Upload your family photos. In five minutes, Kindred generates a beautifully written, watercolour-illustrated children's book featuring your actual family — calibrated to your child's developmental stage, grounded in child psychology, and delivered to your door as a hardcover.

We're not building a photo book. We're building a child development companion — a platform that gets to know your family over time, creating a growing library of books that update as your child grows.

The one-sentence version: Audible for your family's story — but the stories are real, the illustrations are painted, and the writing is shaped by decades of developmental psychology research.

The business

Unit economics (conservative): Hardcover at A$69-79. Subscription at A$8.99/month (one book credit + digital library). Gross margin estimated at 45-55% at volume on print, 80%+ on digital. LTV model: ~A$966 per engaged family before siblings, gifting, or marketplace activity.

Go to market: D2C, built around four compounding loops.

Podcast and creator sponsorships are the primary paid channel — parenting and family lifestyle podcasts offer highly targeted audiences with strong purchase intent. Reach parents in their ears, send them something physical.

Influencer and affiliate programme launches with the product. Books seed naturally into playgroups, school pickups, and grandparents' living rooms. Gifted-first creator seeding moves into a structured affiliate programme with tracked referral links as volume builds.

Referral built into the product moment. When a parent creates a book, they can share it digitally with every family member and friend — grandparents, aunts, uncles, cousins. Each recipient creates a free account to securely view the book, which then lives in their own Kindred library. From there, they start their own Kindred journey. One book becomes the acquisition event for an entire family network.

Subscription as a gifting gateway. Family members who discover Kindred through a shared book are the highest-converting new customers. A "gift a subscription" flow targets the moments that drive this naturally: baby showers, first birthdays, Mother's Day, Christmas. Gifting was mentioned unprompted by 7 of 10 validation interviewees.

Defensibility: Psychology advisory relationships (Prof. Robyn Fivush, Dr. Erica Komisar), LoRA-trained illustration models, proprietary narrative pipeline, and longitudinal family data that compounds over time. The longer a family uses Kindred, the harder it is to replicate their experience elsewhere.

The ask

We're raising a small seed round — under A$250k — from the right people. Not VCs. Family offices, high net worth individuals, and operators who understand consumer products and care about what they're backing.

What the capital is for: Accelerating development and getting to market faster. Specifically: engineering (1-2 additional developers to remove the current single-person bottleneck), LoRA illustration model training, and concierge phase print runs. Critically, we want to establish influencer seeding, affiliate relationships, and organic referral mechanics well ahead of the November-December gifting window — which we expect to be a significant early growth opportunity. A personalised book about a child is one of the most natural Christmas and holiday gifts imaginable, and we want to be ready for it.

Instrument: SAFE note with a 20% discount at the first priced round. We're not assigning a valuation at this stage — that conversation happens after the concierge phase generates real outcome data.

What we're not doing: We're not raising from people who'll want to direct the product. We're raising from people who believe in what we're building and want to be part of it early.
